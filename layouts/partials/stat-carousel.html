• 281 per 100,000

    Oklahoma's female incarceration rate was 281 per 100,000 as of 2018, among the worst in the entire world. If Oklahoma cut that rate in half, it would still be above the national average.

  • 21+%

    Thanks to reform efforts, Oklahoma's Department of Corrections Prison population has declined over 21% in the past five years, with no resulting spike in crime or threat to public safety. We still have the third highest incarceration rate in the nation / world, however, so there's more work to be done.

  • ~$1,300.00

    Oklahoma district courts order defendants to pay an average of around $1,300 per case in court fines and fees, affecting both rural and urban counties. The vast majority of these fines and fees go unpaid, and are a huge barrier to escaping the cycle of incarceration and poverty.
